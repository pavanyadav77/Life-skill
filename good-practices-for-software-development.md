# Software Development Good Practices

### 1. Which point(s) were new to you?
The most helpful new idea for me was **"Over-communication."** I used to think that if I was stuck on a bug or running late, I should just work harder in silence to fix it myself. I didn't realise that staying quiet actually hurts the team's planning. Now I see that sharing my progress—especially when things aren't going well—is a sign of being a professional, not a weakness.

I also liked the tip about joining meetings 5 minutes early. I usually join right on time, but using those extra few minutes to just chat with the team is a great way to build a better relationship with everyone.

---

### 2. Which area do you think you need to improve on? 
The area I need to work on most is **how I ask for help.** Sometimes I just send a message saying "this isn't working" without explaining what I already tried. This wastes my teammates' time because they have to ask me for more details.

**My Ideas to Progress:**
* **The "Show Your Work" Rule:** Before I ask a question, I will spend 15–20 minutes trying to fix it. If I still can't, I will send a message that includes a screenshot of the error and a list of what I’ve already tried.
* **Using Gists:** Instead of pasting long blocks of code into a chat, I’ll use **GitHub Gists**. It makes it much easier for someone else to read my code and help me quickly.
* **Deep Work:** I will keep my phone on silent and away from my desk during my coding hours. I’ve noticed that when a task gets hard, I often get distracted by my phone. Blocking those distractions will help me stay focused and finish my work faster.
